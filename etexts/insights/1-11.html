Microwave Portraits of the entire sky, mapped by NASA’s COBE satellite. Top: raw data--the predominant variations are due to the absolute space motion of the earth; Middle: after motion-compensation, the plane of the galaxy is still in the way. Bottom: having further compensated for the galactic plane, the remaining variations are the fluctuations intrinsic to the Cosmic Background Radiation, which represents the distribution of matter about one billion years after the Big Bang.
